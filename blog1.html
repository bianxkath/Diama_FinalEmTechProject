In today's world, we are constantly bombarded with information from various sources such as social media, news outlets, and the internet in general. It's understandable to ask if we are becoming desensitized to all of the information that is readily available to us. It is possible to view the abundance of information as a good thing. It enables us to keep up with current affairs and educate ourselves about them, and it can also give us a sense of connectedness to the world outside of us. However, there are also negative aspects to consider. It might be challenging to receive and comprehend all of the information that is presented to us at once. This may result in a sense of overwhelm and make it challenging to develop firm ideas or act on the information we receive.

Overall, it's important to be mindful of the information we consume and how it affects us. This might mean limiting our exposure to certain sources or taking breaks from social media. It's also important to fact-check information and critically evaluate what we read or hear to ensure that we don't become desensitized to important issues. It's like a two-sided coin whether the constant bombardment of information can make us desensitized or not. On one hand, it can keep us informed and connected, but on the other hand, it can lead to a feeling of being overwhelmed and can make it difficult to form strong opinions or take action on the information we receive. It's important to be mindful of the information we consume and how it affects us.
